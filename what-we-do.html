Services

We answer the questions your data can’t.

Four practice areas. Every engagement starts from a decision you need to make, not a report we want to sell.

SIG.01 · MARKET MAPPING & SIZING

Know the market’s real shape before you commit to it.

Before you commit a year and a budget to a market, know who actually buys, who sells, what they pay, and where the gaps are. We build the map from primary interviews and named sources, not extrapolated market-size slides.

A question we answered

A logistics-software client asked whether mid-market cold-chain operators in the Gulf justified a dedicated product line. We interviewed 40 operators in three weeks. The answer was yes, but the buyer was the insurer, not the operator. That one finding redirected the entire launch.

SIG.02 · COMPETITIVE INTELLIGENCE

Read your competitor’s next move in the signals they cannot hide.

Companies reveal their strategy months before the press release, in job posts, pricing changes, patent filings, partner moves, and what their own customers say. We read those signals and tell you what they are preparing to do.

A question we answered

A payments firm suspected its main rival was about to undercut on fees. The signal said otherwise: the hiring pattern pointed to a licensing play, not a price war. Our client held pricing, and won the quarter.

SIG.03 · WIN-LOSS & BUYER RESEARCH

Hear why you win and lose, from the people who decided.

The deals you lose rarely tell you why in the CRM. We interview your buyers, the ones who chose you and the ones who did not, and return with the real decision criteria, in their own words.

A question we answered

A cybersecurity vendor was sure it lost on price. Twenty buyer interviews later: price came up twice. “We couldn’t tell who’d pick up the phone after the sale” came up eleven times. They rebuilt the support proof-points in their sales process, and win rate followed.

SIG.04 · SIGNAL MONITORING

A standing watch on the markets you’ve committed to.

Some questions do not end. For markets you are already in, we run a monthly brief of verified changes (new entrants, pricing moves, regulatory drift) and flag the moment something crosses a threshold you set with us.

A question we answered

For a private-equity operating team, our watch flagged a target’s key supplier losing its certification nine weeks before it reached the trade press. They repriced the deal.

Not sure which of these your question is? That’s normal.
